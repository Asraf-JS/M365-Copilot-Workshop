# 06 — Copilot in Outlook

Your proposal is ready. Now you need to send it to your supervisor and manage the conversation that follows. Copilot in Outlook helps you draft emails, summarise threads, and keep up with replies without spending half your day in your inbox.

---

## What Copilot Can Do in Outlook

- Draft emails from a short description or bullet points
- Adjust tone, length, and formality of emails
- Summarise long email threads
- Suggest replies based on the context of a conversation
- Coach you on how your email might land with the reader

---

## Workshop Scenario: Submitting Your Proposal

You'll send your AI Usage Guide proposal to your supervisor for approval, then manage the back-and-forth conversation with your team.

---

## Prompts for Copilot in Outlook

### Drafting the submission email

```
Draft a professional email to my department head submitting 
the AI Usage Guide for approval. Key points to include:
- The guide was developed over the past two weeks
- It covers responsible AI use, approved tools, and dos and don'ts
- I am requesting a review and approval by [date]
- I am available to discuss further if needed

Keep it under 150 words. Formal but not stiff.
```

---

### Following up after no response

```
I sent a proposal email to my supervisor 3 days ago and 
haven't received a reply. Draft a polite follow-up email 
that reminds them about the proposal without sounding pushy.
```

---

### Responding to feedback

Your supervisor replies with questions or changes. Use Copilot to help you respond:

```
My supervisor replied asking me to add a section on 
enforcement and consequences for not following the guideline. 
Draft a reply acknowledging their feedback and letting them 
know I will update the document and resubmit by [date].
```

---

### Communicating with the team

Once approved, you need to let the team know:

```
Draft an email to my team announcing that the AI Usage 
Guideline has been approved by management and will take 
effect from [date]. The tone should be positive and 
encouraging — this is a good thing for the team, not 
a set of rules being imposed on them. Keep it under 
200 words.
```

---

### Summarising a long thread

If the proposal generates a lot of back-and-forth replies, use Copilot to catch up:

In Outlook, open the email thread and click **Summarise** in the Copilot panel:

```
Summarise this email thread. What has been agreed so far, 
what is still unresolved, and what is the next action required?
```

---

### Email coaching

After drafting an email, click **Coaching by Copilot** to get feedback on:
- Tone (does it sound too aggressive, too passive?)
- Clarity (is the request clear?)
- Reader sentiment (how might this email land?)

---

## Tips for Copilot in Outlook

- The **Draft with Copilot** button appears when you start a new email.
- For replies, select the email and look for the **Reply with Copilot** option in the reading pane.
- Copilot can reference the email you're replying to, so it understands the context automatically.
- Thread summaries are especially useful after a holiday or long absence when you need to catch up quickly.
