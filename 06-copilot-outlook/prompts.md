# 06 — Copilot in Outlook: Prompts

Use these prompts to draft, send, and manage the email conversation around your proposal.

> **Prompts to Try:** Open the [copy-paste prompt exercises](./prompts.md) for this topic.

> **Session guide:** All prompts in this topic are used inside Outlook. Use **Help me write** for new emails. Use the **Copilot panel** for thread summaries and coaching. You do not need Copilot Chat for this topic.

---

## Part 1 — Submit the proposal

**Use Help me write in the new email compose window.**

If you saved your handover note from Topic 05, paste it before the prompt below and tell Copilot to turn it into an email:

```
Turn these bullet points into a professional email to my 
department head submitting the AI Usage Guide for approval:

[paste your handover note here]

Keep it under 150 words. Formal but approachable tone.
```

If you did not save the handover note, use this prompt directly:

```
Draft a professional email to my department head submitting 
the AI Usage Guide for approval. Include:
- The guide was developed over the past two weeks
- It covers responsible AI use, approved tools, dos and 
  don'ts, and data privacy obligations under PDPA
- I am requesting a review and approval by [next Friday]
- I am happy to present it in person if helpful

Under 150 words. Formal but approachable tone.
```

---

## Part 2 — Attach and reference the document

After Copilot drafts your email, add a line referencing the attached proposal. You can ask Copilot to include it:

```
Add a sentence near the end of the email mentioning that 
the full proposal is attached as a Word document and that 
a one-page cover summary is included at the front.
```

---

## Part 3 — Coaching before you send

**Before sending, click Coaching by Copilot in the compose window.**

```
Does this email come across as professional and respectful? 
Is the request clear? How might my department head feel 
reading this?
```

Ask Copilot to adjust based on the feedback:

```
Make the tone slightly warmer. It should feel like a 
colleague making a professional request, not a formal 
submission to a committee.
```

---

## Part 4 — Follow up after no response

```
I sent a proposal email to my supervisor 3 working days 
ago and have not received a reply. Draft a polite 
follow-up email that reminds them about the proposal 
without sounding impatient or pushy. Under 80 words.
```

---

## Part 5 — Respond to feedback requesting changes

```
My supervisor replied asking me to add a section on what 
happens if someone misuses AI at work, including 
consequences. Draft a reply:
- Acknowledging their feedback positively
- Confirming I will add the section
- Giving a revised submission date of [date]
- Thanking them for their time

Keep it brief, under 100 words.
```

---

## Part 6 — Respond to approval

```
My supervisor approved the proposal but asked for a few 
minor wording changes before circulating it to the team. 
Draft a short reply:
- Thanking them for the approval
- Confirming I will make the changes
- Letting them know I will share the final version by [date]

Keep it under 60 words. Warm and professional.
```

---

## Part 7 — Announce the approved guideline to the team

```
Draft an email to my full team announcing that the AI 
Usage Guideline has been approved by management and 
takes effect from [date].

Tone: positive and encouraging. This is good news for 
the team, not a policy enforcement announcement.

Include:
- What the guideline covers in one sentence
- Where they can find the document
- Who to contact with questions

Under 200 words.
```

---

## Part 8 — Summarise a long email thread

**Open the email thread in Outlook, then use the Copilot panel.**

```
Summarise this email thread. What has been agreed, 
what is still unresolved, and what action is needed 
from me?
```

If the thread is about the proposal specifically:

```
Based on this email thread, what changes to the AI 
Usage Guide has my supervisor requested? List them 
as a checklist I can work through.
```

---

## Part 9 — Email coaching prompts

Use these after drafting any important email by clicking **Coaching by Copilot**:

```
Does this email sound too pushy? How might the reader 
feel receiving this?
```

```
Is my request clear? What could be misunderstood?
```

```
Is the tone appropriate for emailing a senior manager? 
What would you change?
```

```
Is this email too long? What could be cut without 
losing the key message?
```
