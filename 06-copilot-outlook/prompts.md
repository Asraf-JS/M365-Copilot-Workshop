# 06 — Copilot in Outlook: Prompts

Use these to draft, send, and manage the email conversation around your proposal.

---

## Submitting the proposal

Use **Draft with Copilot** when composing a new email:

```
Draft a professional email to my department head submitting the AI Usage Guide for approval. Include:
- The guide was developed over two weeks
- It covers responsible AI use, approved tools, dos and don'ts, and data privacy
- Requesting review and approval by [next Friday]
- I am happy to present it in person if helpful

Under 150 words. Formal but approachable tone.
```

---

## Following up

```
I sent a proposal to my supervisor 3 working days ago and haven't heard back. Draft a polite follow-up email. Remind them about the proposal without sounding impatient. Under 80 words.
```

---

## Responding to feedback

```
My supervisor replied asking me to add a section on what happens if someone misuses AI. Draft a reply acknowledging the feedback and confirming I will add the section and resubmit by [date].
```

```
My supervisor approved the proposal but asked for a few minor wording changes before we circulate it to the team. Draft a short thank-you reply confirming I will make the changes and share the final version by [date].
```

---

## Team announcement

```
Draft an email to my full team announcing the AI Usage Guideline has been approved and takes effect from [date]. Tone: positive and encouraging. This is good news, not a policy enforcement announcement. Under 200 words.
```

---

## Thread summary

Open a long email thread in Outlook, then ask in the Copilot panel:

```
Summarise this email thread. What has been agreed, what is still unresolved, and what action is needed from me?
```

---

## Email coaching

After drafting any email, click **Coaching by Copilot** and ask:

```
Does this email sound too pushy? How might the reader feel receiving this?
```

```
Is my request clear? What could be misunderstood?
```
