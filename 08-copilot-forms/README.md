# 08 — Copilot in Forms

Your team has been using the AI Usage Guideline for a few weeks. Now you want to know: is it actually working? Copilot in Microsoft Forms helps you build a survey quickly so you can collect structured feedback from your team.

---

## What Copilot Can Do in Forms

- Generate a complete survey from a short description
- Suggest relevant questions based on your topic
- Create different question types: multiple choice, rating scales, text responses, Likert scales
- Help you phrase questions clearly and without bias

---

## Workshop Scenario: Collecting Team Feedback

## Prompts for Copilot in Forms

### Generating your survey

In Microsoft Forms, click **Draft with Copilot** and use a prompt like this:

```
Create a survey to measure how a new AI Usage Guideline 
has impacted employee workflow after one month of use. 

The survey should include:
- Questions about how often they reference the guideline
- Specific tasks where AI has helped or hasn't helped
- A rating scale for overall satisfaction with the guideline
- Open-ended questions for suggestions and concerns
- Questions about confidence in using AI tools

Target audience: non-technical corporate staff in Malaysia.
Aim for 10-12 questions. Mix question types: rating scales, 
multiple choice, and at least 2 open-ended questions.
```

---

### Suggested question types to include

Use Copilot to suggest questions in specific categories:

```
Suggest 3 questions that measure how often employees 
are using AI tools in their daily work. Use a frequency 
scale (Never, Rarely, Sometimes, Often, Always).
```

```
Suggest 2 open-ended questions that encourage honest 
feedback about what is working and what is not working 
with the AI guideline.
```

```
Create a Net Promoter Score style question asking how 
likely employees are to recommend using Copilot to a 
colleague, on a scale of 0-10.
```

---

## Recommended Survey Structure

Here is a suggested structure you can use as a reference:

**Section 1: Usage (3-4 questions)**
- How often do you use AI tools in your daily work?
- Which Microsoft 365 apps do you use Copilot in most frequently?
- How often do you refer to the AI Usage Guideline?

**Section 2: Impact (3-4 questions)**
- Rate how much Copilot has improved your productivity (1-5 scale)
- Which task has benefited the most from using Copilot?
- Has the guideline changed how you approach using AI? (Yes / No / Somewhat)

**Section 3: Confidence and Support (2-3 questions)**
- How confident are you in using AI tools responsibly at work? (1-5 scale)
- Do you feel you received enough training to use Copilot effectively?
- What additional support would help you use AI more effectively?

**Section 4: Open Feedback (1-2 questions)**
- What is working well since the guideline was introduced?
- What would you change or improve about the guideline?

---

## Tips for Forms with Copilot

- Keep surveys under 15 questions. Response rates drop significantly after that.
- Always include at least one open-ended question — the qualitative responses often reveal things that rating scales miss.
- Use consistent scales across the survey (don't mix 1-5 with 1-10 without good reason).
- Preview the form before sharing to check the flow and wording.
- In Forms, you can set the survey to collect responses anonymously — this usually gives more honest feedback.

---

## Exporting Responses to Excel

Once responses are collected (or you're using the sample data):

1. In Microsoft Forms, open your survey
2. Click the **Responses** tab
3. Click **Open in Excel**

This exports all responses into an Excel file, with each question as a column and each respondent as a row. This is what you'll analyse in the next topic.
