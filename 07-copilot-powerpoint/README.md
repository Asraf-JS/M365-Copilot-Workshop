# 07 — Copilot in PowerPoint

Your proposal has been approved. Now you need to communicate the AI Usage Guideline to your team. A presentation is the clearest way to do this, and Copilot in PowerPoint can build it directly from your Word document.

---

## What Copilot Can Do in PowerPoint

- Generate a full presentation from a Word document or a text prompt
- Add slides, rewrite slide content, and adjust layouts
- Suggest speaker notes for each slide
- Summarise presentations
- Help you change the overall tone or style

---

## Workshop Scenario: Presenting to Your Team

You're turning the approved AI Usage Guide proposal into a presentation that you'll deliver to your department. The goal is to help your team understand the guideline, feel positive about using AI, and know exactly what's expected of them.

---

## Prompts for Copilot in PowerPoint

### Creating a presentation from your Word document

In PowerPoint, click **Create presentation from file** and reference your proposal document:

```
Create a presentation from this AI Usage Guide proposal. 
The audience is my department team — they are non-technical 
staff who are new to using AI at work. 

Structure the presentation as follows:
1. Title slide
2. Why we created this guideline
3. What AI tools are approved for use
4. The key dos and don'ts (one slide each if needed)
5. Data privacy — what to watch out for
6. How this benefits our daily work (3 examples)
7. Next steps and support available
8. Q&A slide

Add speaker notes to each slide with talking points.
```

---

### Adding and improving slides

```
Add a slide after the title that starts with a short 
scenario: "Imagine you need to write a monthly report 
in 10 minutes instead of 2 hours..." Use this to 
show the practical benefit of AI before diving into 
the guideline.
```

```
The Dos and Don'ts slide has too much text. 
Break it into two slides and use icons or short 
phrases instead of full sentences.
```

```
Add a slide that visually explains the difference 
between the free Microsoft Copilot and the paid 
Microsoft 365 Copilot. Use a simple comparison layout.
```

---

### Improving speaker notes

```
Rewrite the speaker notes for slide 4 (Dos and Don'ts). 
I want to explain the reasoning behind each point in 
plain language, and include one real-world example 
I can share with the team verbally.
```

---

### Adjusting tone and design

```
This presentation feels too formal. Rewrite the slide 
titles and bullet points to use a more conversational 
and encouraging tone. The team should feel motivated, 
not like they're reading a policy document.
```

```
Suggest a colour theme and slide layout that feels 
modern and professional, suitable for an internal 
corporate presentation.
```

---

### Summarising an existing presentation

If you have an existing presentation you want to review before the meeting:

```
Summarise this presentation. What are the 3 main 
messages the audience will take away?
```

---

## Tips for Copilot in PowerPoint

- The better your Word document, the better the generated presentation. Clean headings and clear sections in Word translate directly into well-structured slides.
- Copilot will apply your organisation's PowerPoint template if one is set up in your Microsoft 365 tenant.
- Speaker notes are often the most valuable output — even if you adjust the slides, keep the notes as a script reference.
- Use **Designer** (also in PowerPoint) alongside Copilot to improve visual layout after the content is generated.
