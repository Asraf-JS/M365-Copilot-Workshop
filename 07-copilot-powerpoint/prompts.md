# 07 — Copilot in PowerPoint: Prompts

Use these to build your team presentation from the approved AI Usage Guide.

---

## Creating from a Word document

In PowerPoint, click **Create presentation from file** and reference your proposal doc:

```
Create a presentation from this AI Usage Guide proposal. The audience is my department team — non-technical staff who are new to using AI at work.

Structure:
1. Title slide — include a tagline
2. Why we created this guideline (1 slide)
3. What AI tools are approved (1 slide)
4. Key dos and don'ts (1-2 slides)
5. Data privacy — what to watch out for (1 slide)
6. How this helps your daily work — 3 concrete examples (1 slide)
7. What happens next and where to get help (1 slide)
8. Q&A slide

Add speaker notes to every slide with talking points I can use when presenting.
```

---

## Adding and improving slides

```
Add a slide after the title that opens with a short scenario: "Imagine it's Monday morning and you have 3 reports to write before lunch..." Use it to show the practical benefit of AI before getting into the guideline content.
```

```
Slide 4 has too much text. Break it into two slides. Use short phrases, not full sentences. Keep each bullet to 8 words or less.
```

```
Add a slide that compares free Microsoft Copilot vs paid Microsoft 365 Copilot using a simple side-by-side layout. Show 3 key differences only.
```

---

## Improving speaker notes

```
Rewrite the speaker notes for the Dos and Don'ts slide. I want to be able to explain the reasoning behind each point verbally, with one real example I can share with the team.
```

```
The speaker notes for the Data Privacy slide are too technical. Rewrite them so I can explain the key point in 2 minutes to staff with no IT background.
```

---

## Tone and design

```
The slide titles feel too formal and policy-like. Rewrite all slide titles to sound more conversational and engaging, while still being professional.
```

```
Suggest a colour theme for this presentation that feels modern and aligns with a Malaysian corporate environment. Describe the colours and the rationale.
```

---

## Summarising before a meeting

Open your presentation and ask:

```
Summarise this presentation in 5 bullet points. What are the 3 things the audience must walk away knowing?
```
